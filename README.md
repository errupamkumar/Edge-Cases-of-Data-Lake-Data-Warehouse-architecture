# Edge-Cases-of-Data-Lake-Data-Warehouse-architecture
**Here's how to think about the four domains and their relationship to your platform:**

Platform ops KPIs are the ones your data engineering team owns directly. End-to-end stream latency and Kafka consumer lag are the early-warning system — they tell you something is wrong before any business metric degrades. The DLQ rate is especially important when you're evolving schemas across Debezium, Flink, and ClickHouse simultaneously.
Product KPIs like ETA accuracy and P95 wait time are what the business actually cares about — but they're derived entirely from your stream processing. The fact that Flink computes P95 wait time in 5-minute tumbling windows per zone is what makes surge pricing decisions possible in near real-time. These KPIs also serve as an integration test for the whole pipeline: if ETA accuracy degrades, you look at GPS anomaly rate, then Flink checkpoint health, then Kafka lag — working backwards through the stack.
Driver supply KPIs are the least obvious but often the most valuable. Driver utilisation is the "Goldilocks" metric — too low means supply is wasted, too high means you'll get wait time spikes within minutes. GPS anomaly rate is a data quality KPI masquerading as a supply KPI — it directly tells you whether your state in Flink (last known position, running distance) is trustworthy.
Revenue and fraud KPIs require the highest data integrity because errors here have direct financial consequences. The CDC reconciliation delta is set to zero-tolerance — any non-zero value means Debezium dropped events, which is a payment audit failure. This is exactly why Bronze is immutable: when you find a CDC gap, you can patch Silver from Bronze without guessing.
